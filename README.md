# Scoliosis Care Centers Replacement Landing Page

This repository contains a production-ready static replacement page for `https://scoliosiscarecenters.com/`, built as a warmer, parent-centered, SEO-focused healthcare landing page.

## Current Page Audit Summary

### Existing strengths

- Clear home-page positioning around **Non-Surgical Scoliosis Treatment for Children and Teens**.
- Strong conversion actions already exist: **See Results**, **Contact Us**, **Start Your Free Case Review**, and **Schedule Your ScoliSnap™ Now**.
- Meaningful trust signals are present, including service longevity since 2008, San Jose origins, Oklahoma City expansion, worldwide family reach, Dr. Matthew Janzen, DC, and testimonials.
- The original page includes highly relevant service terms: MRI-guided bracing, SOSORT-guided bracing and rehab, scoliosis screenings, scoliosis monitoring, ScoliSnap™ Screen and Monitor, curve correction, rib hump improvement, and posture transformation.
- The page contains multiple testimonials with names and patient/parent context.

### Existing SEO weaknesses

- The existing H1 focuses on non-surgical scoliosis treatment, but the page does not fully align its structure around parent search intent such as pediatric scoliosis treatment, scoliosis care for children, and scoliosis brace support.
- Several strong keyword themes are present but scattered across dense sections rather than organized into scannable H2/H3 blocks.
- Contact information, location references, and services are available but could be better structured for LocalBusiness and MedicalBusiness schema.
- FAQ intent is not fully captured on the home page even though parents are likely to search practical questions before contacting a provider.
- Image alt text exists in the crawled page, but image assets should be mapped and optimized for explicit parent-centered sections.

### Existing UX weaknesses

- The tone can feel intense or sales-forward in places, especially around surgery prevention and worsening language.
- Parent reassurance is not the first emotional message, even though the target visitor may be anxious.
- Many proof points and claims are grouped together, making it harder for a parent on mobile to scan calmly.
- The journey from concern → options → process → proof → next step can be clearer.
- The embedded form fallback text appears on the crawled page and may distract users if JavaScript fails.

### Recommended primary keyword

- **pediatric scoliosis treatment**

### Recommended supporting keywords

- scoliosis treatment for children
- non-surgical scoliosis treatment
- scoliosis care for children and teens
- scoliosis brace treatment
- scoliosis brace support
- scoliosis therapy for kids
- scoliosis screening for children
- scoliosis monitoring
- ScoliSnap screening
- Silicon Valley Scoliosis Method
- MRI-guided scoliosis bracing

### Recommended SEO title and meta description

- **SEO title:** Pediatric Scoliosis Treatment & Non-Surgical Support | Scoliosis Care Centers
- **Meta description:** Compassionate pediatric scoliosis treatment and non-surgical support for children and teens, including bracing, rehab, monitoring, screenings, and free case review guidance.

## Likely Search Intent

The likely primary intent is a parent researching **non-surgical scoliosis treatment options for a child or teen**, often before or after receiving a diagnosis or brace/surgery recommendation. The parent wants to understand whether a provider can help, what the process looks like, whether the approach is credible, and how to take a low-pressure next step.

### Asset access note

Direct shell requests to the original domain returned `403 Forbidden`, and Playwright browser installation also failed with `403 Forbidden`, so original image binaries could not be downloaded into `/assets/` in this environment. The page preserves the original image meanings with `data-original-image` attributes and an `assets/original-image-map.json` file. If production WordPress media access is available, replace the two SVG placeholders and testimonial initials with the original media files.

## SEO Implementation Checklist

- **H1:** `Pediatric scoliosis care that helps parents find a calmer path forward`
- **H2s:** Support for child journey, how we help, what to expect, why parents choose the approach, stories, early detection, locations, FAQ, final CTA.
- **Meta title:** `Pediatric Scoliosis Treatment & Non-Surgical Support | Scoliosis Care Centers`
- **Meta description:** Included in `index.html`.
- **Canonical URL:** `https://scoliosiscarecenters.com/` placeholder/live canonical included.
- **Open Graph tags:** Title, description, type, URL, and site name included.
- **Schema markup:** MedicalBusiness, LocalBusiness, WebPage, and FAQPage JSON-LD included.
- **Alt text:** All local SVG images include descriptive alt text and embedded SVG title/description.
- **Internal link opportunities:** About, Services, Contact, Privacy Policy, Success Stories, Results, ScoliSnap scheduling, and existing free case review form.
- **FAQ section:** Included with safe, non-guarantee answers.
- **Mobile optimization:** Mobile-first responsive grid, accessible navigation toggle, sticky header, large tap targets.
- **Page speed considerations:** Static HTML/CSS, minimal JavaScript, local SVG placeholders, lazy loading for non-hero image, no unnecessary external dependencies.

## Final Notes for Clinical, Legal, and SEO Review

- The original page includes strong medical/performance claims such as `100% Surgery Prevention Rate — For curves 30 degrees and under` and statements about curve reduction. These should be reviewed by a clinician and legal/compliance reviewer before publication.
- The original language `harmful scoliosis X-rays` was not reused verbatim because it may be medically sensitive and fear-based; the page uses `radiation-free standing MRI monitoring` instead.
- Testimonials are preserved and trimmed for readability. Confirm permissions and exact wording before publication.
- The page uses `© 2008-2026` because this build was created in 2026; adjust if the live site requires a different footer year.
- Replace local SVG placeholders with the actual WordPress media files once asset access is available.
- Adding provider profile details for Dr. Matthew Janzen, DC and Dr. Mike Janzen, if confirmed on the live site, would strengthen E-E-A-T.
- Adding exact appointment URLs and verified local Google Business Profile links would improve conversion and local SEO.
